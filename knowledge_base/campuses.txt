Metropolia UAS Campuses and Future Plans (Detailed Overview)

Myllypuro Campus (Helsinki)
- Opened in 2019, largest campus of Metropolia.
- Fields: Health care, Social services, Construction, Well-being, and (in the future) Business studies.
- Facilities:
  - Modern simulation labs for nursing, physiotherapy, radiography, and other health professions.
  - Construction and civil engineering workshops.
  - Library and study spaces.
  - Cafeteria and student lounge areas.
  - Direct connection to Myllypuro metro station.
- Community hub: designed for collaboration with local residents, companies, and city services.
- Future: Business programs will be relocated here from Myyrmäki.

Arabia Campus (Helsinki)
- Located in Arabiaranta, known as the “Campus for Creative Minds.”
- Fields: Culture, Creative industries, Design, Media, Arts.
- Facilities:
  - Studios for design, film, and media production.
  - Workshops for fine arts, ceramics, and crafts.
  - Sound and video editing labs.
  - Exhibition spaces for student projects.
  - Library specialized in arts and culture.
- Strong ties with Helsinki’s cultural institutions and creative companies.

Myyrmäki Campus (Vantaa)
- Known as the “Campus for Green Technology and Business.”
- Fields: Technology, Engineering, Business (until relocation).
- Facilities:
  - Engineering laboratories for mechanical, electrical, and environmental technology.
  - Business classrooms and entrepreneurship hubs.
  - Research labs for sustainable energy and green innovation.
  - Library and IT support services.
  - Cafeteria and sports facilities.
- Future: Business studies will move to Myllypuro; technology programs remain until further consolidation.

Karamalmi Campus (Espoo)
- Known as the “Campus for Smart Solutions.”
- Fields: ICT, Engineering, Smart technologies.
- Facilities: 
  - ICT labs for software development, cybersecurity, and data analytics.
  - XR Center (Extended Reality) for virtual and augmented reality projects.
  - Engineering workshops and prototyping labs.
  - Collaboration spaces for startups and research projects.
  - Library and student services.
- Strong industry connections with Espoo’s technology companies.
- Future: ICT and engineering programs remain here until further restructuring.

Summary of Future Changes
- Business programs → move from Myyrmäki to Myllypuro.
- Technology and ICT → remain in Myyrmäki and Karamalmi until consolidation.
- Goal: concentrate operations on fewer campuses, modernize facilities, and strengthen collaboration between fields.
