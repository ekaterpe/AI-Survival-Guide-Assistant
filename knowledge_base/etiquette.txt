Finnish Study Etiquette for International Students

 Teacherâ€“Student Relations
- Teachers are addressed by their first name, not with formal titles.
- It is not customary to use formal "Mr./Mrs." or "Sir/Madam" forms.
- The relationship is based on equality and mutual respect.
- Students are encouraged to ask questions freely and challenge ideas.

Classroom Behavior
- Students may leave the classroom quietly (e.g., to use the restroom) without asking permission.
- Eating or drinking during lectures is generally acceptable, as long as it is not disruptive.
- Punctuality is valued, but occasional lateness is tolerated if explained.
- Active participation is encouraged, but silence is also respected.

 Independence & Responsibility
- Finnish education emphasizes self-directed learning.
- Students are expected to manage their own schedules and deadlines.
- Group work is common, but individual responsibility is always highlighted.
- Trust is placed in students to complete tasks without strict supervision.

 Communication Style
- Direct and honest communication is appreciated.
- Teachers and students interact informally, often in a friendly manner.
- Feedback is constructive and aimed at improvement, not criticism.
- Respect for personal space and privacy is important.

 Equality & Inclusion
- Hierarchy is minimal: teachers and students are seen as equals in the learning process.
- Diversity and inclusion are actively promoted.
- International students are encouraged to share their perspectives and cultural backgrounds.

 Summary
Finnish study etiquette is informal, respectful, and based on trust. Students enjoy freedom in the classroom, are treated as equals by teachers, and are expected to take responsibility for their own learning.
