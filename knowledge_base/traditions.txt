Student Festivals, Traditions and Customs in Finland and Metropolia UAS

 Vappu (May Day)
- Date: 1 May.
- One of the biggest student celebrations in Finland.
- Traditions:
  - Students wear their white graduation caps (ylioppilaslakki).
  - Parades, picnics, and parties in parks (especially Kaivopuisto in Helsinki).
  - Drinking sima (homemade mead) and eating munkki (donuts).
- For students: Vappu is a symbol of youth, freedom, and community.

 Freshman Initiation (Fuksiaiset)
- Organized by student associations at the beginning of autumn semester.
- New students (“fuksit”) participate in playful tasks, games, and challenges.
- Purpose: integration into student community, meeting peers and tutors.
- Often ends with a party and awarding of “fuksi points.”

 Sitsit (Academic Dinner Parties)
- Traditional student dinners with singing, speeches, and humor.
- Rules:
  - Songs from student songbooks are sung together.
  - Toasts and playful punishments for breaking etiquette.
- Common in student unions and associations, especially in engineering and arts programs.

 Student Overalls (Haalarit)
- Colorful overalls worn by students, each program has its own color.
- Decorated with patches from events, parties, and associations.
- Symbol of belonging to a student community.
- Worn during festivals, parties, and national student events.

 Annual Student Events
- Orientation Week: games, tours, and social activities for new students.
- Wellbeing Week: workshops on health, stress management, and sports.
- International Day: multicultural fair with food, music, and presentations.
- Graduation Ceremonies: formal events with speeches and awarding diplomas.

 Special Features
- Informality: Finnish student traditions are playful and relaxed.
- Equality: everyone participates, regardless of background.
- Community: traditions strengthen bonds between students, associations, and alumni.
- Creativity: costumes, songs, and humor are central to celebrations.

 Summary
Student traditions in Finland and Metropolia UAS include Vappu, freshman initiation, sitsit dinners, and the use of colorful student overalls. These customs emphasize community, equality, and joy, making student life vibrant and unique.
